CS491: Space Science Algorithms (791*)

Semester: 2nd Semester
Prerequisites: CS315

Course Description

This course introduces algorithms used in space science, with a focus on remote sensing and radio astronomy. Students work with real-world data from the MODIS satellite sensor and explore radio interferometry techniques used in modern radio telescopes. Emphasis is placed on practical implementation and analysis of large-scale scientific datasets.

Instructor: Trienko Grobler

Learning Outcomes

  • Understand the principles of remote sensing and satellite data processing
  • Process and analyse data from MODIS and similar satellite sensors
  • Understand the fundamentals of radio interferometry
  • Implement calibration and imaging algorithms for radio telescope data
  • Apply scientific computing techniques to large astronomical datasets
  • Evaluate and interpret results from space science algorithms
  • Communicate findings through written reports and practical demonstrations

Assessment

  • Assignments: 20%
  • Test A: 30%
  • Test B: 30%
