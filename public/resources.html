Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Where are my course materials and assignments?
All course materials are available on the SUNLearn portal. You should have access automatically at the start of each semester. If you can't access a course, contact the lecturer or undergrad@cs.sun.ac.za.
Q: When are office hours and how do I book a consultation?
Faculty office hours are listed in each course syllabus. Most lecturers offer drop-in hours or appointments. Email the lecturer to confirm their availability. For academic advising, contact undergrad@cs.sun.ac.za.
Q: What programming languages and tools will I need?
The core languages in the CS curriculum include Python, Java, and C/C++. Most tools are free and available for Linux, Windows, and macOS. Your course materials will specify exactly which tools you need for each course. Campus computers have all required software pre-installed.
Q: How do I access the campus computing labs?
Computing labs are open during posted hours. Your student ID grants access. Hours and locations are posted at the CS Building entrance (A50x corridor). Labs close during semester breaks. For access issues, contact IT Support.
Q: What if I'm struggling with my courses?
Don't hesitate to reach out for help! Resources include:
  • Attend lecturer office hours
  • Join peer study groups
  • Contact undergrad@cs.sun.ac.za for tutoring referrals
  • Visit the counseling center for academic planning
Q: How do I apply for honours or master's programmes?
Contact postgrad@cs.sun.ac.za for application deadlines and requirements. Typically, applications open in the last semester of your degree. You'll need a strong academic record and may need to propose a research topic or supervisor.
Q: Are there internship or industry partnership opportunities?
Yes! The division has partnerships with local and international technology companies. Check the news page and Facebook group for internship announcements. The Career Services office also maintains a list of opportunities.
Q: What is the academic integrity policy?
Stellenbosch University has strict academic integrity standards. Plagiarism, cheating, and unauthorized collaboration are serious violations. You'll receive detailed guidelines in each course. When in doubt, ask your lecturer. The university provides resources on proper citation and academic writing.
Q: How can I get involved in research?
Opportunities to participate in research include:
  • Honours project (required, supervised research)
  • Master's thesis (independent research)
  • Join research groups as an undergraduate
  • Attend departmental research seminars
  • Contact lecturers during office hours about research opportunities
